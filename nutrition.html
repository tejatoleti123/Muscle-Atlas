YOUR NUTRITION GUIDE

PERFORMANCE STRATEGY

The molecular foundation of strength. Understanding the chemistry behind elite hypertrophy, ergogenic aids, and systemic recovery.

SCIENTIFIC DISCLAIMER & CAUTION

The information provided in the Nutrition Guide is for educational and knowledge purposes only. Performance nutrition and supplementation should always be discussed with a qualified healthcare professional or clinical nutritionist. Do not substitute this information for professional medical advice. Always prioritize Whole-Food sourcing and systemic safety.

1. THE SURVIVAL FOUNDATION (REAL FOOD)

Think of your Body like a Supercar

Before talking about fancy powders and supplements, we must talk about the 90% that actually builds your physique: Real Food. If you don't feed the engine correctly, it won't matter how much Nitro (Pre-workout) you use—the car won't move.

Proteins: The Bricks

Think of protein as the bricks of a building. Every time you lift weights, you create tiny cracks in your muscles. Protein fills those cracks to build a bigger, stronger wall.

Best Sources: Eggs, Chicken, Soya, Paneer, Dal/Lentils.

Carbs: The Petrol

Carbohydrates are your fuel. Without them, you will feel tired and weak in the gym. Your brain and muscles need this glucose to fire at 100% capacity.

Best Sources: Rice, Roti/Bread, Potatoes, Oats, Bananas.

Fats: The Engine Oil

Fats don't make you "fat"—they manage your hormones (like Testosterone). They keep the "engine" running smoothly and protect your joints.

Best Sources: Ghee, Nuts, Seeds, Avocado, Whole Eggs.

2. CREATINE MONOHYDRATE: THE ATP ENGINE

Scientific Mechanism

Creatine is the most researched ergogenic aid in history. It primarily works by increasing the stores of Phosphocreatine in your muscles, which is used to produce new ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)—the primary energy currency for high-intensity movement.

Key Benefits

  • Strength & Power: Superior ATP regeneration allows for 1-2 extra reps at peak intensity.
  • Cell Volumization: Draws water into the muscle cell (not under skin), increasing protein synthesis.
  • Neuroprotection: Emerging research shows significant cognitive and brain-health benefits.

Dosage: 5g daily, indefinitely. No loading phase required for long-term users.

3. THE PRE-WORKOUT MATRIX

N.O. Boosters (The Pump)

L-Citrulline Malate: Increases nitric oxide production.

Benefit: Vasodilation leading to better nutrient delivery and the 'pump' sensation.
Dose: 6-8g of Pure Citrulline.

Stimulants (Focus)

Caffeine Anhydrous: Antagonist to adenosine receptors.

Benefit: Reduced perceived exertion and increased neural drive.
Side Effects: Jitters, Elevated HR, Sleep disruption.

Buffers (Endurance)

Beta-Alanine: Elevates Carnosine levels in muscle.

Benefit: Buffers lactic acid (H+ ions), delaying muscle failure.
Side Effect: Paresthesia (The harmless 'tingling' sensation).

4. THE ANABOLIC WINDOW

The Myth of the 30-Minute Window

While "post-workout nutrition" is vital, the metabolic window is actually 4-6 hours wide. The priority is Total Daily Protein Intake and reaching the Leucine Threshold (3g+ per meal) to trigger mTOR signaling for muscle protein synthesis.

BCAAs vs. EAAs

BCAAs on their own are incomplete. You require all 9 Essential Amino Acids (EAAs) to build muscle. Only use BCAAs if training in a fasted state to prevent extreme catabolism.

5. SYSTEMIC RECOVERY

The 0.00$ Supplement: Sleep

No amount of Creatine can outweigh the damage of chronic sleep deprivation. During Stage 3/4 (Deep Sleep), Growth Hormone (GH) spikes and Cortisol (Stress hormone) is regulated. Without 7-9 hours of quality sleep, your body remains in a catabolic (muscle-wasting) state regardless of your diet.

6. PRO TIP: SUPPLEMENT HIERARCHY

"Supplements are the 5% that tops off the 95% of hard training, calorie-tracking, and sleep. Never prioritize a pre-workout over a high-quality protein source or a consistent training program. Master the basics, then optimize the margins."
