Arms (Biceps & Triceps)

PRECISION LEVERS & FORCE

1. Muscle Fundamentals

Anatomical Breakdown

  • Triceps Brachii: Three-headed muscle making up a massive 2/3 of upper arm girth.
  • Biceps Brachii: Two-headed muscle spanning the anterior arm responsible for the peak.
  • Brachialis: Small, incredibly deep muscle that actually pushes the bicep outward.
  • Forearms: An extreme complex network of over 20 flexors and extensors dictating absolute grip.

Primary Biomechanics

The arms act as the primary kinetic levers for all upper body loading permutations. Triceps are physically responsible for Elbow Extension (pushing), while Biceps solely dictate Elbow Flexion (pulling mechanics).

2. Movement Patterns

Elbow Flexion

Any curling isolation motion. Supinated grips target the inner/short bicep head, while neutral hammer grips heavily target the brachialis and outer forearm meat. (e.g., Dumbbell Curls, Hammer Curls)

Elbow Extension

Extending the arm aggressively against heavy resistance. Heavy close-grip pressing builds structural mass, while absolute extensions hyper-target the long head. (e.g., Skullcrushers, Cable Pushdowns)

3. Essential Exercises

Isolation

Barbell Bicep Curls

The undisputed king of total massive bicep development.

  • Stand tall, gripping the heavy bar roughly shoulder-width.
  • Keep elbows entirely pinned to your sides.
  • Curl the weight strictly upward, squeezing the biceps at the absolute peak.
  • Lower the weight under intense 3-second eccentric control to full extension.

COMMON MISTAKES

  • Swinging the torso to generate cheat momentum.
  • Letting the elbows drift forward, turning it into an anterior delt raise.
  • Failing to fully extend the arm at the bottom to stretch the fibers.

WHEN TO USE

Utilized right at the start of an arm block to handle maximum mechanical weight.

Isolation

Skull Crushers (Lying Tricep Ext)

Ruthlessly targets the long head of the triceps via overhead tissue stretch.

  • Lie flat on a sturdy bench holding an EZ bar suspended over your chest.
  • Bend exclusively at the elbows, lowering the bar behind your forehead.
  • Keep your upper arms totally stationary throughout the entire eccentric.
  • Violently press the bar back up to the exact starting lock.

COMMON MISTAKES

  • Flaring the elbows outward excessively (inviting tendonitis).
  • Moving the shoulders physically back and forth (turning the movement into a lat pullover).
  • Lowering the bar too fast and risking extreme elbow destruction.

WHEN TO USE

The mandatory heavy core movement for producing absolute thick tricep horseshoe development.

Isolation

Cable Tricep Pushdowns

Relentless constant tension for the lateral and medial triceps heads.

  • Attach a thick rope or V-bar to the highest cable pulley point.
  • Tuck elbows firmly and violently into your side ribs.
  • Push the attachment downward, ripping the rope apart at the bottom for an extreme squeeze.
  • Control the heavy weight back up to exactly 90 degrees, no higher.

COMMON MISTAKES

  • Using downward torso bodyweight to press the cable down.
  • Letting the elbows travel upward as the cable rises, losing the isolation.
  • Releasing the tension instantly at the bottom.

WHEN TO USE

Executed in the middle to end of a workout sequence for maximum blood pooling and metabolic exhaustion.

Isolation

Preacher Curls

Total isolation of the short head by physically restricting the humerus.

  • Sit securely on the bench, pinning your triceps into the pad.
  • Grip the EZ bar and unrack it slowly.
  • Curl upwards, squeezing tightly at the peak of contraction.
  • Lower the bar back down without ever hyperextending the elbow.

COMMON MISTAKES

  • Leaning back wildly as you pull.
  • Failing to achieve a deep stretch.
  • Relying on forearm flexion.

WHEN TO USE

Beginner requirement for sculpting dramatic peak width.

Compound

Close-Grip Bench Press

Absolute heavy mechanical overload for the lateral and medial triceps.

  • Lie flat, gripping the bar exactly shoulder-width.
  • Tuck elbows tightly at a 30-45 degree angle.
  • Lower the bar to your lower sternum/upper abs.
  • Press violently straight up, locking the elbows completely.

COMMON MISTAKES

  • Setting the grip too narrow and destroying the wrists.
  • Flaring the elbows like a standard bench press.

WHEN TO USE

Intermediate massive strength builder driving pure tricep horsepower.

4. Advanced Protocols

Advanced Hypertrophy Sweep

- Close-Grip Bench: 4x6-8 (Heavy Load)
- Barbell Curls: 4x8-10
- Skull Crushers: 4x10-12
- Incline DB Curls: 3x12 (Deep Stretch)
- Rope Pushdowns: 3x15 (Drop Set Finish)

Arm Annihilation Supersets

- A1. Overhead Cable Extensions: 4x12 A2. DB Hammer Curls: 4x12
- B1. Tricep Dips: 3xFailure B2. Preacher Curls: 3x12
- C1. Diamond Pushups: 3xFailure C2. Reverse EZ Bar Curls: 3x15

5. Hypertrophy Science

Direct arm training involves strict isolation of the delicate elbow joint through two antagonistic force movements. Because arms endure massive indirect trauma on chest and back days, they require a meticulous balance of heavy stimulus and high-volume isolation. Aim for 10-14 direct sets exclusively for arms on an isolated day to spark maximal localized hypertrophy.

6. Critical Failures

  • Swinging the Torso: Utilizing lower back swinging to generate momentum on heavy bicep curls entirely shifts the targeted weight off the bicep belly, risking lumbar damage while achieving zero actual arm growth.
  • Excessive Elbow Flare: Allowing the elbows to flare wildly outward on tricep extensions instantly displaces mechanical tension away from the triceps and onto the anterior deltoid and delicate elbow connective tissues.
  • Avoiding the Stretch: Failing to achieve a deep, tearing tissue stretch on the long heads. If you completely avoid incline DB curls (biceps) or overhead extensions (triceps), your arm peaks will perpetually remain underdeveloped.

7. PRO TIP: The Long Head Secret

"To achieve absolute maximal arm circumference, prioritize movements where your arm is completely overhead (for triceps) or extended deeply behind your body (for biceps). These specific geometric angles hyper-target the long heads of each muscle, which comprise the absolute vast majority of visible arm mass."
